# Mathematical Appendix: A₀ and RP for Autoregressive Transformer LLMs (LLaMA‑class)

## How to Read This Appendix
This appendix provides a **formal, theorem–proof** foundation for the $A_0$ invariant and its operationalization as Reality Protocol (RP) in **discrete autoregressive transformer** language models.

---

## 1. Preliminaries and Notation

### 1.1 Tokenization and Sequences
Let $\mathcal{V}$ be a finite vocabulary, and let $\text{EOS} \in \mathcal{V}$. Output is $x_{1:T}$ with $x_t \in \mathcal{V}$.

### 1.2 Autoregressive Model
An autoregressive model defines conditional distributions:
$$\pi_\theta(x_t \mid x_{<t}) \in \Delta(\mathcal{V})$$
For LLaMA‑class, it is a deterministic mapping to logits $\ell_t \in \mathbb{R}^{|\mathcal{V}|}$, then $\pi_\theta(x_t \mid x_{<t}) = \text{softmax}(\ell_t / \tau)$.

### 1.3 State Space
Define generation state as $S_t := (x_{<t}, h_t)$. Let $\mathcal{S}$ be the set of reachable states.

---

## 2. Decoding as a Controlled Markov Process
### 2.1 Controlled Transition Kernel
$$P(x_t=a \mid S_t) = \Pi(a \mid S_t)$$
where $\Pi$ is the effective policy. $\{S_t\}$ is a **controlled Markov process**.

### 2.2 Termination as an Absorbing Set
$\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{term}} := \{ S : \text{last token is EOS} \}$.
**Assumption A (Absorption):** Once EOS is emitted, the process remains terminated.

---

## 3. Admissibility and Local Cost (RP Core Objects)
### 3.1 Admissible Actions
$\mathcal{A}(S) = \mathcal{V}$. Define $\mathcal{A}'(S) := \{ a \in \mathcal{A}(S) : a \text{ satisfies admissibility at } S \}$.

### 3.2 Local Transition Cost
$$\Xi(S,a) := \alpha Z(S,a) + \beta H(S,a) + \gamma T(S,a)$$
with $\alpha,\beta,\gamma \ge 0$.

### 3.3 RP Local Relaxation Rule
$$a^*(S) \in \arg\min_{a \in \mathcal{A}'(S)} \Xi(S,a)$$
When $\mathcal{A}'(S)=\varnothing$, RP collapses to termination.

---

## 4. The A₀ Invariant (Discrete Form)
**A₀ (Minimal Local Discharge, Discrete).** A generated trajectory $\{S_t\}$ is **A₀‑consistent** if at each step $t$, $x_t$ is an admissible local minimizer of $\Xi$ at $S_t$.

---

## 5. Stability Theorem: A₀ as Lyapunov‑Style Descent
### 5.1 Potential Function
Define nonnegative **instability potential** $\Phi : \mathcal{S} \to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}$.
**Assumption B:** $\Delta\Phi(S,a) := \mathbb{E}[\Phi(S') \mid S, a] - \Phi(S)$, and $\Xi$ is **monotone aligned** with $\Delta\Phi$.
**Assumption C:** Strict progress when possible.

### 5.2 Stable Set
$$\mathcal{S}_* := \{ S \in \mathcal{S} : \forall a \in \mathcal{A}'(S),\; \Delta\Phi(S,a) \ge 0 \} \cup \mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{term}}$$



---

## 6. Main Theorem
**Theorem 1.** Under Assumptions A–C, any trajectory generated by RP satisfies:
1. $\Phi(S_t)$ is a supermartingale until $\mathcal{S}_*$: $\mathbb{E}[\Phi(S_{t+1})\mid S_t] \le \Phi(S_t)$.
2. The trajectory reaches $\mathcal{S}_*$ in finite time with probability 1.
3. Deviating policies yield strictly higher expected $\Phi$ and are unstable.

---

## 7. Corollaries
* **Corollary 1 (Non‑Selection):** Transitions are not “selected”; high $\Xi$ trajectories fail to persist.
* **Corollary 2 (No‑Agency):** Evolution is fully specified by $(\mathcal{S},\mathcal{A}',\Xi,\Pi)$.
* **Corollary 3 (Bounded Gen) & 4 (Silence):** Termination is an absorbing stable state.

---

## 8. Mapping to LLaMA‑Class Transformers
Locality, stochasticity, and admissibility (via masks/penalties) directly fit the abstraction.
Operational instantiations for $H(S,a)$, $Z(S,a)$, $T(S,a)$ merely need to preserve ordering alignment (Assumption B).

---

## 9. Prompt‑Level RP as an Attractor (Soft-Field Approximation)
Prompt-level RP induces implicit cost shaping:
$$\Pi_{\mathrm{RP}}(a\mid S) \propto \Pi_0(a\mid S)\,\exp(-\lambda\,\widehat\Xi(S,a))$$
Theorem 1 applies conditionally: the process converges to low-instability attractors.
